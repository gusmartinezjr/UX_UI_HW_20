Introduction

Problem Statement

The Voices for Children of SA website is designed to have a user-friendly experience that offers the community a clear understanding of the VoC SA’s mission. We have observed that the website doesn’t headline the goal which is identifying critical child issues to the community leaving them guessing where they can help which is causing a decline in volunteer and donor support. How might we improve the website so that volunteers and donors will help VoC SA achieve its mission of responding to current and emerging needs of children?

The Solution

The goal of this project was to create a responsive website for the 'Voices of Children' organization to help improve donor and volunteer support, clearly communicate the mission statement, and show the community impact.

    User Research: Story Board


    Analyzing both the quantitative and qualitative data, I was able to form a fundamental understanding of the users' pain points they were facing. I then storyboarded Bernard Price to highlight the discovered pain points and possible solutions.

    User Research: "I Like, I Wish & What If"


    In order to find the best solutions for the issues the website was facing, we utilized the “I Like, I Wish and What If” exercise and dot voted the best solutions to move forward.

    Ideation


    Our first prototype was on InVision. This gave us the foundation we needed to visualize the user flow from a mobile website point of view. Then the updated mid fidelity wireframes were created in Figma for user testing.

    Usability Testing & Findings

    We tested our mid fidelity prototype on five users giving them four tasks to complete. We had a success rate of 89%. This gave us the data to add process improvements on the high fidelity prototype.

    With the user research and testing, we were able to iterate our design to help solve the problem that online users were facing locating the donation and volunteer areas of the non-profit website.
